## Why the choice of site changes everything for a conch piercing

The **conch piercing** goes through the conch, the central cartilage of the ear, in its inner or outer part depending on placement. It is one of the most popular cartilage piercings for the sculptural look it offers, alone or as part of an ear composition. Its popularity has grown steadily in recent years, driven by the multi-piercing curations showcased by studios.

The choice of buying site matters as much as the choice of jewelry itself. A conch piercing jewel stays in place throughout the healing period, which lasts 6 to 12 months on cartilage according to a study published in the *Journal of the American Academy of Dermatology* in 2018. Buying from a site that traces its alloy and ships fast reduces the risk of complications and hidden fees.

### The three criteria of a reliable site

- **Titanium traceability**: the product page must explicitly mention ASTM F136 or Ti-6Al-4V ELI, not just "surgical titanium". This nuance determines the absence of releasable nickel.
- **Format suited to the conch**: a straight labret for a standard conch, a clicker or hoop for an orbital conch. The bar is most often chosen in 1.2 mm diameter.
- **Origin and delay**: a French site avoids customs fees and the 7 to 14 import days that add to the price shown on American shops.

For more on the material question, the article on [which metal to choose for a piercing](/en/blog/what-metal-to-choose-for-piercing/) details the differences between titanium, gold and surgical steel for cartilage.

## How to choose your conch piercing jewelry site

The decision comes down to four questions: is the jewel available in France to avoid customs and delay, is the titanium properly certified ASTM F136 for the healing phase, does the format match the type of conch (standard or orbital), and does the total budget including shipping and customs stay on target. A simple decision grid helps choose between Nébuleuse Bijoux or Cranky for the accessible starter jewel, and Madlords or Maria Tash for the high-end renewal piece.

### The three mistakes to avoid

1. Buying a jewel described as "surgical titanium" without an ASTM F136 or Ti-6Al-4V ELI mention, which may be a non-implantable industrial grade.
2. Choosing a set gold piece as the initial starter jewel, when a healing conch tolerates a heavy or non-implant-grade decorative jewel poorly.
3. Ignoring customs fees on orders outside the European Union, which can add 30 to 40% to the listed price of American brands.
