## Why the studio choice matters so much for a navel piercing

The **navel piercing** is one of the body piercings most exposed to complications. Its location, subject to clothing friction, sweat and abdominal movement, makes it a long-healing piercing: 6 to 12 months depending on anatomy. The studio chosen in Paris determines three critical elements: anatomical placement, infection risk and the initial bar metal.

According to the Association of Professional Piercers (APP), a navel poorly placed relative to the upper skin fold is among the leading causes of migration and rejection. A professional piercer assesses the depth of the fold, the skin's hold and directs you toward a suitable curved bar, whereas a non-specialized salon often places a generic, badly sized bar.

### The objective criteria for choosing a navel studio

Four criteria structure this comparison and allow you to judge a studio without being swayed by its marketing:

- **Piercing technique**: only the single-use sterile hollow needle is APP-compliant for the navel. The gun is banned, it crushes the tissue and increases infection risk.
- **Initial bar metal**: implant-grade ASTM F136 titanium and nickel-free 14-carat gold are the only metals validated for the healing phase, which can last up to a year for a navel.
- **Anatomical reading**: the piercer must measure the skin fold and choose a bar length suited to initial swelling, then offer a shorter bar once healing is advanced.
- **Hygiene protocol**: class B autoclave, individual bar in a sterile blister, sterile gloves, chlorhexidine disinfection.

## Practical tips for a successful navel piercing in Paris

Beyond the studio name, a few common-sense checks eliminate bad addresses and secure healing.

- Check that the studio pierces exclusively with a needle, never a gun for the navel.
- Ask to see the initial bar in its sterile blister before the procedure.
- Confirm the metal is ASTM F136 titanium or nickel-free 14-carat gold, not a low-grade surgical steel.
- Check that the piercer measures the skin fold and sizes the bar accordingly.
- Book ahead on weekends: serious studios refuse peak-hour walk-ins to avoid rushing.

### Mistakes to absolutely avoid

1. **Choosing a gun beauty salon**. The gun crushes the tissue instead of piercing it cleanly, which lengthens healing and multiplies infection risk on an already slow-healing area.
2. **Accepting an uncertified steel or plated initial bar**. For a navel that heals up to a year, the metal must be inert. Low-grade steel and plating cause frequent reactions.
3. **Removing the initial jewelry too soon**. A navel heals in 6 to 12 months. Changing the bar before the end causes micro-lesions and restarts healing. For daily aftercare follow-up, the [ear piercing guide](/en/blog/ear-piercing-guide/) details transposable protocols.
