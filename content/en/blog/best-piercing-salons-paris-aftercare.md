## Why follow-up is the real criterion for a successful first ear piercing

A **first ear piercing** is a commitment of several months, not just the 15 minutes of the procedure. A lobe heals in 6 to 8 weeks, cartilage takes 4 to 6 months. According to the Association of Professional Piercers (APP), 70 percent of piercing rejections and migrations result from poorly chosen anatomical placement or a metal unsuited to the healing phase. Both mistakes are decided at the moment the salon is chosen.

Piercing follow-up covers three distinct moments. First, the aftercare instructions given on the day of the procedure, which shape daily hygiene. Then downsizing, meaning the replacement of the longer initial bar with a shorter piece once swelling has gone down, generally between 4 and 8 weeks. Finally, the ability to come back with a question or have a red spot checked without waiting three weeks for an appointment.

### Objective criteria for comparing Paris salons

Five criteria make it possible to evaluate **piercing salons in Paris** without being swayed by marketing:

- **Piercing technique**: only a sterile single-use hollow needle complies with APP standards. Guns should be ruled out, including for lobes.
- **Initial jewelry metal**: ASTM F136 implant grade titanium or nickel free 14k gold, the only metals validated for healing. Alloy details are covered in our guide on [what metal to choose for a piercing](/en/blog/what-metal-to-choose-for-piercing/).
- **Hygiene protocol**: class B autoclave, individually blistered needles, sterile gloves, skin disinfection before the procedure.
- **Quality of initial advice**: anatomical reading of the ear, placement choice, clear information on healing times.
- **Follow-up accessibility**: opening hours, walk-in returns, downsizing done on site, healing jewelry available.

## Practical tips for a successful first ear piercing in Paris

A few simple checks help rule out bad addresses before sitting in the chair.

- Check that the salon pierces exclusively with needles, including for lobes.
- Ask to see the initial jewelry in its sterile blister before the procedure.
- Confirm the metal is ASTM F136 titanium or nickel free 14k gold, the word "titanium" alone is not enough.
- Ask about downsizing at the first appointment: a serious salon anticipates and schedules it.
- Favor a salon open all week, a doubt about redness should never wait 10 days.

### Mistakes to avoid for a first piercing

1. **Choosing a beauty salon that uses a gun**. A gun crushes tissue instead of piercing it, extends healing by 30 to 50 percent and triples the infection risk according to a 2023 APP study.
2. **Ignoring follow-up when choosing**. A low price with no easy way to return costs more at the first healing problem.
3. **Accepting initial jewelry in gold plate or an unknown alloy**. During healing the metal must be inert, or allergic reactions follow.
