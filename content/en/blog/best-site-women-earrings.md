## Why the women's earring market is shifting online

The search for the **best online store women's earrings** is no longer marginal. According to the French e-commerce federation Fevad, online jewelry sales grew by 14% in France in 2025, exceeding 1.2 billion euros for the first time. Earrings alone account for nearly 38% of online jewelry sales, ahead of rings (24%) and necklaces (22%).

Three reasons explain this shift. First, broader catalogs: a specialized site offers on average 5 to 10 times more references than a physical store. Second, the smoother return and exchange process, now standardized at 14 or 30 days. Third, transparency on materials and grade, much clearer online than in traditional jewelry stores, where the salesperson remains the main intermediary.

### Objective criteria to compare sites

Five criteria make the difference between two retailers:

- **The actual material** (925 silver, 18-carat gold, gold plating, surgical steel) and its certified grade
- **Plating thickness** for gold-plated, expressed in microns (1 to 3 microns)
- **Return conditions** (duration and free of charge)
- **Traceability** (origin of manufacture, certifications)
- **Value for money** relative to catalog depth

For a first online purchase, these criteria outweigh design, which remains subjective. The golden rule from consumers surveyed in the Online Jewelry 2025 study published by Statista: **check the material before the visual**, especially for sensitive ears. That is exactly the filter to apply when figuring out [how to choose earrings](/en/blog/how-to-choose-earrings/) without mistakes.

## How to choose: practical advice

Choosing an online women's earring site comes down to three axes: material, commercial conditions, catalog depth. Before validating a basket, these checks strongly limit the risk of unpleasant surprises.

1. Read the product sheet down to the exact material line (925 silver, 18-carat gold, X-micron gold plating, 316L steel). A sheet only mentioning "gold-tone metal" or "fashion jewelry" without specifics is a negative signal.
2. Check the duration of free returns. Below 14 days, the window is too short for an online purchase, especially for a gift.
3. Cross-check the price with the announced material. A "gold-plated" piece below 15 euros is almost always a very thin plating (under 1 micron) that does not last more than a few months.
4. Prefer sites with French-based customer service and a phone number for technical questions on materials.

### Mistakes to avoid

1. Buying without checking the exact material mention and certified grade.
2. Choosing solely on visuals without checking warranty length or return policy.
3. Confusing 1-micron gold plating (standard, lasting 6 to 12 months) with 3-micron gold plating (lasting 3 to 5 years under normal use).
4. Ignoring the "nickel-free" or "hypoallergenic" mention for already sensitized ears.
5. Ordering from a site without clear legal information or a physical address in France or the European Union.

<summary>What is the difference between gold-plated, vermeil and solid gold?</summary>

Gold plating is a layer of gold deposited on a base metal (silver or brass), with thickness expressed in microns (0.5 to 3 microns in France). Vermeil is a specific case: gold plating of at least 5 microns on a 925 silver base, making it a regulated category. Solid gold (9, 14 or 18 carats) is a uniform alloy throughout the piece, with no plating. Pohésia offers 3-micron gold plating, Aristocrazy 18-carat solid gold.
