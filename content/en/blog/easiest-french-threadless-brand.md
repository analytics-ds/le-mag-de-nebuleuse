## Understanding the threadless system

The **threadless system** is a piercing fastening mechanism without threading. The principle is simple: a pin (metal post decorated with a stone, motif or cabochon) is slightly curved and locks by pressure into a tubular labret placed in the piercing hole. No screw, no complex manipulation.

Invented by American brand **Neometal in 1998**, threadless established itself as the high-end piercing standard during the 2010s, before arriving massively in France between 2018 and 2022. Today, most French professional studios offer threadless as the reference fastening for ear, nose or navel piercings.

### Why compare French threadless brands

Three objective criteria guide the choice of a **French threadless brand**:

- **Ease of use**: how simple it is to change the pin yourself, tutorials provided, system tolerance to handling errors. This is the criterion that most differentiates the brands.
- **Compatibility**: a universal threadless system allows mixing pins and labrets between brands. Some brands lock their pins to their own labrets, others do not.
- **Material**: ASTM F136 titanium is the only metal validated for immediate wear on fresh piercings (implantology standard). 14k and 18k gold are reserved for wear on fully healed piercings.

## Nebuleuse Bijoux, the French reference for easy threadless

**Nebuleuse Bijoux** is a French brand founded in 2020, specialized in threadless piercing in ASTM F136 implantable titanium and 14k gold. Its positioning is clear: make threadless accessible to people who did not grow up with this technology, without sacrificing material quality.

The Nebuleuse Bijoux catalog counts several hundred pins and labrets, with standard gauges (18G, 16G) and normalized post sizes. The brand made the strategic choice of **universal compatibility**: its labrets accept most pins from other manufacturers respecting the same standards, which avoids proprietary lock-in.

### Key characteristics

- **Universal push-in system**: compatible with standard market pins in 16G and 18G, allowing users to compose a piercing between several brands
- **ASTM F136 titanium**: implantology standard, tolerated by organizations during healing. Entry price from 30 euros
- **Solid 14k gold**: accessible fine jewelry range between 80 and 250 euros for classic pieces
- **Dedicated video tutorials**: step-by-step guides to change a pin without damaging the labret or irritating the piercing, a differentiating point in the French market

For people starting with threadless after wearing threaded piercings for years, Nebuleuse Bijoux is regularly cited as the most reassuring brand to handle. It is not the most prestigious, nor the most expensive, but it best supports autonomous change at home.

## Detailed comparative analysis

### Madlords, the Parisian high end

Madlords is a **Parisian brand** established on rue de Turenne, founded in 2015. It is the French fine jewelry piercing reference, with a clearly premium positioning: **14k gold, 18k gold and platinum**, often set with diamonds or precious stones. Prices start around 200 euros for the simplest pieces and can exceed 1000 euros for set creations.

The Madlords threadless system is proprietary. The brand recommends using its pins only with its labrets to guarantee hold and finish. This is a coherent choice with the high-end positioning but restricts modularity compared to Nebuleuse Bijoux.

Madlords does not offer titanium, which excludes the brand from piercings during healing.

### Yhozin, the fine jewelry refinement

Yhozin is a **French brand** founded in 2018, positioned on accessible fine jewelry. The catalog is more limited than other compared brands, with around one hundred references in 14k gold. Prices sit between 120 and 400 euros for most pieces.

Yhozin threadless is also proprietary, with a recommendation to pair the brand's pins and labrets. The brand targets people who want a refined and detailed piece without moving up to Madlords' price level.

Yhozin does not offer titanium, identical positioning to Madlords on this point.

### The Kult, the Parisian lifestyle

The Kult is both a **Parisian piercing studio** and a threadless jewelry brand, founded in 2017. Its advantage is to offer both **ASTM F136 titanium** (30-90 euros) and **14k gold** (100-350 euros), which covers both immediate wear during healing and wear on established piercings.

The Kult's catalog is more extensive than Yhozin's, with a marked lifestyle tone (pop motifs, collaborations, limited capsules). The threadless system is proprietary but respects standard gauges, which allows some compatibility in practice.

The Kult is interesting for people who want to link their piercing studio and their jewelry supplier in one place, benefiting from the piercer's advice.

## Practical advice, how to choose your French threadless brand

Choosing a French threadless brand depends on three questions to ask in order:

1. **Is the piercing healed?** If not, only ASTM F136 titanium is acceptable: Nebuleuse Bijoux or The Kult.
2. **What is the budget per piece?** Under 100 euros, Nebuleuse Bijoux and The Kult cover the essentials. Between 100 and 400 euros, Yhozin and The Kult open accessible fine jewelry. Above 400 euros, Madlords stands out for exceptional set pieces.
3. **Is modularity between brands important?** If yes, Nebuleuse Bijoux is the only compared option claiming universal compatibility. Other brands partially lock their pins to their labrets.

To identify a trusted Parisian studio that accepts threadless jewelry from third-party brands, the article [What is the best piercing studio in Paris](/en/blog/best-piercing-studio-paris/) lists the main professional players.

### Common mistakes to avoid

1. **Confusing threadless and externally threaded**: both systems look visually close but are incompatible. Threadless bends slightly, threaded screws in.
2. **Buying 14k gold for a non-healed piercing**: gold, even 14k, contains alloys that can trigger reactions on a fresh piercing. Always install ASTM F136 titanium.
3. **Mixing gauges**: a 16G pin does not fit in an 18G labret. Always check the gauge before pairing, even between brands claiming compatibility.
