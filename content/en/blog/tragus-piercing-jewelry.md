## Why the choice of tragus jewelry matters

The **tragus** is the small protruding cartilage in front of the ear canal. Its healing takes 4 to 8 months on average, far longer than a simple lobe. Throughout that period, the jewelry stays in constant contact with an open wound, which makes the material decisive to avoid inflammation and rejection.

Choosing **tragus piercing jewelry** is therefore not just about looks. Shape matters as much as metal: a labret, with its flat back plate, limits friction in the hollow of the ear, where a poorly fitted ring irritates the healing area.

The Association of Professional Piercers, the reference body, recommends exclusively implant-grade ASTM F136 titanium, solid gold of at least 14 carats without nickel, or medical glass for a healing piercing. **Gold plating** and low-grade surgical steel release nickel, the leading cause of contact dermatitis according to public data.

### Criteria to compare a tragus jewelry shop

- Stated material standard (ASTM F136 for titanium, carat rating for gold)
- Availability of labrets suited to the tragus, in several bar lengths
- Bar gauge offered (1.2 mm in 16G is the most common, sometimes 1.0 mm in 18G)
- Manufacturing origin and traceability
- Shipping cost and lead time within mainland France

## How to choose your tragus jewelry

Five criteria settle the decision quickly. The first is the material: implant-grade ASTM F136 titanium or solid gold of at least 14 carats for a healing tragus, without exception. The second is the shape: a flat-back labret is more comfortable than a ring during the first months. The third is bar length, to be planned slightly longer at first to absorb the initial swelling. The fourth is the gauge, usually 1.2 mm in 16G. The fifth is the return policy, ideally 14 to 30 days.

| Phase | Recommended material | Suggested shape | Indicative budget |
|-------|----------------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Healing (0 to 8 months) | ASTM F136 titanium | Flat-back labret, longer | 10 to 34 euros |
| Transition (8 to 12 months) | F136 titanium or 14k gold | Fitted labret | 18 to 42 euros |
| Permanent (healed) | 14 or 18-carat gold | Set labret or fine ring | 30 to over 47 euros |

### Mistakes to avoid

1. Buying gold plating for a fresh tragus. Plating wears and releases the base metal, a source of irritation in a slow-healing area.
2. Choosing a ring too early. On a healing tragus, a ring moves and irritates, where a labret stays stable.
3. Taking a bar that is too short. The swelling of the first weeks requires a margin of 1 to 2 millimeters.
4. Ignoring the material standard. Jewelry sold as "titanium" without an ASTM F136 mention may contain non-medical alloys.
