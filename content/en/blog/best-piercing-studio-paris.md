## Why choosing the right piercing studio really matters

The chosen **piercing studio** determines three critical elements: healing, infection risk and final aesthetic quality. According to the Association of Professional Piercers (APP), 70 percent of piercing rejections and migrations result from a poorly defined anatomical placement or a metal unsuited to the healing phase. A lobe presents little difficulty. A cartilage, daith or industrial requires precise anatomical reading and jewelry adapted to the initial swelling.

### Objective comparison criteria

Four criteria structure this comparison and make it possible to evaluate a studio without being influenced by marketing communication:

- **Piercing technique**: only the single-use sterile hollow needle complies with APP standards. The piercing gun is banned for cartilage and strongly discouraged for the lobe.
- **Initial jewelry metal**: ASTM F136 implant grade titanium and 14k nickel free gold are the only metals validated for the healing phase, which lasts from 2 to 12 months depending on the area.
- **Piercer training and certification**: APP membership or a long apprenticeship with a recognized piercer is worth more than a generic aesthetics diploma.
- **Hygiene protocol**: class B autoclave, individually blistered needles, sterile gloves, chlorhexidine skin disinfection.

It is the criterion that separates a serious studio from a beauty salon. ## Practical tips for choosing a piercing studio in Paris

Beyond the brand name, a few common sense checks help eliminate bad addresses.

- Verify the studio uses needle exclusively, not gun, including for the lobe.
- Ask to see the initial jewelry in its sterile blister before the procedure.
- Confirm the metal is ASTM F136 titanium or 14k nickel free gold, not just low-end surgical steel.
- Read recent Google reviews, especially recent negative comments that reveal structural problems.
- Book ahead on weekends, serious studios refuse rush hour walk-ins to avoid rushed work.

### Mistakes to absolutely avoid

1. **Choosing a gun-based beauty salon for a lobe**. The gun crushes tissue instead of piercing it, which prolongs healing and increases infection risk.
2. **Accepting a gold plated or bronze initial jewelry**. For healing, the metal must be inert. Gold plated, bronze and copper alloys cause frequent allergic reactions.
3. **Trusting an abnormally low price**. A 25 euros cartilage piercing covers neither ASTM F136 titanium jewelry nor a complete sterile protocol. It signals a compromise on hygiene or metal.
