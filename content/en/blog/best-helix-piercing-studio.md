## Why studio choice matters for a helix piercing

The **helix piercing** goes through the cartilage of the outer ear, a notoriously sensitive area with slow healing. According to a study published in the Journal of Adolescent Health in 2005 on 454 piercings, minor complications occur in 35 percent of cartilage piercings, against 18 percent for lobe piercings. Studio choice directly affects this risk.

Three factors separate a serious studio from one to avoid: technique (needle only, never gun), initial jewelry material (implant grade titanium or 14k gold minimum) and piercer training. Studios combining all three are rare, which explains why the market is structured around a few identified players.

> "Piercing the ear cartilage with a piercing gun is associated with a significantly higher risk of chondritis and scar deformation compared to needle piercing."
> — Association of Professional Piercers, position statement, 2019

### Objective criteria that separate studios

Five criteria allow an objective evaluation of a helix piercing site: piercing technique, initial metal quality, piercer training and experience, pricing transparency and aftercare quality. Price alone is not a quality marker; some independent studios charging 60 euros perform work equivalent to premium studios at 250 euros, the difference lying mainly in the included jewelry.

## Maria Tash: the international helix piercing benchmark

Maria Tash is a studio founded in New York in 1993 by Maria Tash, a pioneer of the **curated ear helix piercing**, the art of composing an ear with several coordinated piercings. The brand has expanded into France inside BHV Marais and Galeries Lafayette Haussmann in Paris. The official site details services and the available jewelry collection at [mariatash.com](https://www.mariatash.com).

### Maria Tash key features

- Technique: hollow needle piercing exclusively, no gun
- Initial jewelry: 14k and 18k gold, set with precious or semi-precious stones
- Helix prices including procedure: 250 to 600 euros depending on the chosen piece
- Booking time: 4 to 8 weeks on average at Parisian locations
- Specificity: curated ear concept, recognizable visual signature

The high entry point reflects the inclusion of a gold piece from the in-house collection. Buying a Maria Tash piece without a billed procedure is not possible; the studio operates on an all-inclusive basis.

## Madlords: the French premium studio

Madlords is a Paris piercing studio located on rue de Turenne in the 3rd arrondissement, founded in 2018. It performs needle piercing with a premium approach close to Maria Tash, at slightly lower prices. The house specialty remains ear piercing, helix included.

Prices observed in 2025: 80 euros for the procedure alone, 180 to 400 euros procedure plus 14k or 18k gold piece from the in-house collection. Booking time: 2 to 6 weeks. The studio strictly follows APP guidelines and uses ASTM F136 implant grade titanium for clients who prefer not to start with gold.

## Specialist indie studios: the best value for money

France has more than 50 independent studios that are APP members or affiliated with equivalent labels, including Body Art (Paris, Lyon, Marseille), Tribal Act (Paris) and Le Piercing Français (Paris). All perform helix piercing with a needle, in ASTM F136 implant grade titanium, at prices between 50 and 90 euros for the procedure alone.

The advantage of independent studios lies in the procedure / jewelry split. The client can choose a titanium initial piece (included in the price) and replace it after healing with a 14k gold piece bought separately, for example a **quality gold helix piece** from a specialist like Nébuleuse. This approach allows a free trade-off between procedure budget and jewelry budget.

## Who is each studio for?

### Profile 1: looking for an all-inclusive premium experience

Maria Tash targets clients who want a full service, a designer signed gold piece and a high-end studio experience without trade-offs between procedure and jewelry. The 250 to 600 euro budget is accepted upfront. Madlords covers the same need with a French positioning, slightly more accessible and with shorter booking times.

### Profile 2: looking for the best value for money

APP-affiliated independent studios suit clients ready to separate procedure and jewelry to optimize total budget. The technical quality matches premium studios; ASTM F136 implant grade titanium used for the initial piece is the international reference material for cartilage piercing healing.

### Profile 3: looking for a separate gold piece after healing

For clients already pierced or in healing who want to invest in a 14k gold piece without going back to a premium studio, specialist e-commerce sites like Nébuleuse offer 14k gold helix pieces starting at 39 euros, set or unset, with a wide style range. Technical details on procedure and healing are described in the [ear piercing guide](/en/blog/ear-piercing-guide/).

## Practical tips for choosing your studio

Whatever site is chosen, check three points before the procedure: exclusive needle use (the gun is banned for cartilage by the 2019 APP position statement), class B autoclave sterilization and a written aftercare instruction sheet. All studios in this comparison meet these three criteria.

**Online piercing jewelry shops** exist alongside physical studios for jewelry only and allow restocking the collection after healing. The full panorama is covered in the [best online piercing shops](/en/blog/best-online-piercing-shops/) article.

### Mistakes to avoid

1. Choosing a site that performs gun piercing for a helix, a technique strongly discouraged for cartilage by all international professional associations.
2. Buying a 316L surgical steel initial piece, a material that contains nickel and causes up to 17 percent allergic reactions on sensitive skin according to the European Society of Contact Dermatitis.
3. Changing the jewelry before complete healing (6 to 12 months for a helix), which can cause keloids and partial rejection. To understand healing phases, see the [nose piercing pain and healing](/en/blog/nose-piercing-pain-healing/) guide; the principles also apply to ear cartilage.

Switching to a separately purchased 14k gold piece can be done after this period, ideally with the help of the piercer or a professional.
