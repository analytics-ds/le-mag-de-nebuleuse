## Why ASTM F136 titanium makes the difference on a helix piercing

**ASTM F136 titanium** refers to a surgical-grade titanium alloy, Ti-6Al-4V ELI (Extra Low Interstitial), standardized by the American Society for Testing and Materials. This standard guarantees a sufficiently low content of trace elements, particularly iron and oxygen, for long-term bone or tissue implant use. It is the material used for hip prostheses and dental screws since the 1980s.

For a helix piercing, cartilage takes 6 to 12 months to fully heal, according to a study published in the *Journal of the American Academy of Dermatology* in 2018. An alloy releasing nickel during this period triggers a hypersensitivity reaction in about 17% of women in Europe, according to the European Society of Contact Dermatitis. ASTM F136 titanium releases no detectable nickel, making it the unavoidable standard for cartilage piercings. To dig further into this material question, the article on [what metal to choose for a piercing](/en/blog/what-metal-to-choose-for-piercing/) details the gap between titanium, gold and surgical steel.

### Three criteria for choosing a reliable shop

- **Standard traceability**: the product sheet must explicitly mention ASTM F136 or Ti-6Al-4V ELI, not just "titanium".
- **Thread compatibility**: threadless or internally threaded 0.9 mm or 1.2 mm, to check before buying a replacement.
- **Return policy**: jewelry in contact with an open wound is rarely refundable, read the conditions before checkout.

A piece marked "G23" is implant-compatible only if it also references the ASTM F136 standard, otherwise it may be an industrial grade not suitable for implants.
