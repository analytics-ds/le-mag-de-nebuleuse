Trending body jewelry for women is no longer limited to festival season or summer evenings. Since the spring-summer 2026 runway shows, belly chains, body necklaces and anklets have moved into everyday wardrobes. This revival is driven by a confident aesthetic of body enhancement, championed by accessible brands and independent French designers.

This guide covers the types of **trending body jewelry** to adopt this season, the best materials to choose from, and styling tips for wearing them daily without missteps.

## What Is Body Jewelry?

Body jewelry refers to any accessory worn on the torso, hips, legs or shoulders, excluding the face and hands. The category is broad: pieces slipped directly against the skin or layered over clothing to structure a silhouette.

For a complete introduction to the world of body jewelry, from vocabulary to first pieces to choose, the [beginner's guide to body jewelry](/en/blog/body-jewelry-beginner-guide/) provides a solid foundation before exploring current trends.

The main families of body jewelry:

- **Belly chains and waist chains**: worn at the waist or on the hips
- **Body necklaces**: drape over the décolleté or sternum
- **Back jewelry**: visible on bare backs, usually in fine chain
- **Anklets**: worn solo or layered
- **Chest jewelry**: harnesses, sternum chains
- **Thigh chains**: high chains worn around the thighs

### Belly Chains and Waist Chains

The belly chain remains the standout piece in this category. In 2026, preferred styles are ultra-thin (under 2 mm wide), in 925 silver or gold-plated steel. Designers are working with adjustable lengths for a high (at the waist) or low (at the hips) fit. Geometric-motif chains and micro rhinestone pavés account for the majority of new releases.

The waist chain worn visibly over a midi dress or a linen set is one of the key looks of spring 2026.

### Body Necklaces and Back Jewelry

The body necklace descends below the neckline and can be layered or worn alone over a V-neck top. Some styles feature a central pendant (teardrop, cross, star) that follows the sternum line.

Back jewelry is seeing a resurgence of interest driven by backless dresses and open-back cuts in ready-to-wear collections. These pieces clip at the upper back and descend in a fine chain to the lower back.

### Anklets

The anklet is back under the influence of resort and festival trends. The layering technique applies here too: two or three anklets of different widths worn on the same leg create a depth effect without overloading the look. Rope models with silver clasps sit alongside fine chains with charms.

### Chest Jewelry

Body harnesses, long confined to rock or fetish aesthetics, now come in minimalist versions: two or three fine chains linked across the shoulders and chest. These pieces work under a linen blazer or over a tank top for a subtle layered effect.

## Popular Materials for Body Jewelry

The choice of metal determines the durability and comfort of body jewelry, particularly for pieces worn against the skin.

**925 silver**: the reference metal for body jewelry. Its natural shine suits all skin tones and its hypoallergenic composition reduces skin reactions. Note that silver tarnishes on contact with perspiration: regular upkeep is necessary.

**Stainless steel**: a more affordable and highly durable option. 316L steel is hypoallergenic and rust-proof, making it the best choice for summer or beach body jewelry. For body piercings like the navel, surgical-grade stainless steel is recommended — [choosing a quality navel piercing online](/en/blog/buy-quality-belly-button-piercing-online/) details the criteria to check before buying.

**Gold-plated**: 14-carat gold-filled finishes deliver a luxurious look at a lower price. Durability varies with plating thickness (opt for a minimum of 2.5 microns). Avoid prolonged sweating and contact with sunscreen to preserve the finish.

**Implant-grade titanium**: the preferred metal for any jewelry in prolonged contact with sensitive skin. Lightweight, inert and available in anodised (coloured) versions, it suits reactive skin types.

## Styling Body Jewelry for Everyday Wear

The strength of body jewelry lies in its versatility. A few core principles for incorporating it into a daily outfit:

**Visual hierarchy rule**: one statement piece at a time. If the belly chain is elaborate (multi-strand, rhinestones), keep the other jewelry minimal (fine ring, small hoop earrings).

**Body jewelry layering**: stacking multiple pieces in the same zone (two belly chains at different lengths, three anklets) creates depth without overloading the look. Gold and silver mixing is now widely accepted when the proportions feel cohesive.

**Calculated visibility**: back jewelry only works if the garment reveals it. Opt for backless dresses, open-back tops or cut-out swimwear. Similarly, the belly chain pays off when visible over a crop top or a slit dress.

**Care**: remove body jewelry before swimming in the sea or chlorinated pool, applying sunscreen or doing intense physical activity. A rinse in fresh water and gentle drying extends the life of each piece.

## Body Jewelry Spring-Summer 2026: The Looks to Adopt

Spring-summer 2026 body jewelry trends revolve around three main directions:

**The "invisible" aesthetic**: ultra-thin chains, almost imperceptible at first glance, that enhance the body without weighing it down. This style pairs perfectly with the wave of sheer fabrics and see-through looks.

**The return of subtle rhinestones**: micro crystal pavés on steel or silver pieces produce a light-catching effect without ostentation. Distinct from Y2K-era rhinestones (coloured, oversized pieces): here, everything is small and geometric.

**Anatomical jewelry**: ready-to-wear designers are drawing from jewelry that mimics human forms. In accessible brands, this translates to teardrop, rib or spiral pendants that follow the body's natural curves.

## Choosing Body Jewelry Based on Body Type

Body jewelry is not reserved for specific body shapes. A few adjustments help find the most flattering pieces:

**Lean or athletic figures**: multi-strand belly chains and structured harnesses add volume and definition. Wide designs (three or more strands) work well here without overpowering the silhouette.

**Hourglass or waist-defined figures**: a simple waist chain naturally accentuates the curve. Opt for adjustable chains for a precise fit. Long back jewelry (reaching the lower back) flatters this body type by highlighting the bust.

**Straight or broad-shouldered figures**: hip chains worn low create the illusion of a defined waist and hips. Belly chains with swinging charms at hip level add movement and dynamism without rigidity.

**All body types**: the anklet is the most versatile piece of body jewelry. Fine or stacked, it draws the eye toward the feet and visually lengthens the leg, whatever the frame.
